How the score works. The mobility-stress score (0–100) is a congestion-weighted composite: Traffic congestion ×2, plus population density ×1 and transit gap ×1 (inverse of metro/bus access), divided by 4. Component scores are an indicative assessment grounded in documented evidence (e.g., Silk Board ~4.5 km/h at peak; the ORR tech corridor's gridlock; current Namma Metro line coverage), not precise per-zone measurements. Zone boundaries follow the well-known BBMP 8-zone framework; note that BBMP has since been reorganised into five city corporations under the Greater Bengaluru Governance Act. Treat scores as a debate-starter and prioritisation aid, not an official index.
